The 3 R's

Reducing the use of non-biodegradable products will lower plastic pollution and eventually have an impact on land pollution. This is why it is very important to reuse and recycle every possible item.It will help in lessening the burden of landfills. Moreover, it will save more natural resources

Reforestation

Reforestation involves replanting an area with trees. It will further prevent floods and soil erosion. Trees help to control soil erosion as they act as an obstacle to wind speed from carrying soil sediments with themselves. Moreover, it will make the land more fertile and enhance biodiversity.

Efficient Agricultural Practices

Excessive use of chemical insecticides, pesticides and weedicides results in acidification of the soil.In addition to contaminating the soil, they also do no good to the crops. Therefore, farmers must use natural fertilizers such as manure and go for natural pest management strategies to secure the quality of the soil.Crop rotation method must be adopted to maintain the health of the soil.It will control the impact on the cultivated environment.

Proper Waste Management

Be it for industrial or household waste, efficient waste disposal is one of the most effective ways of curbing land pollution.Many industries dump their waste in open areas that damage the land. Industrial waste is composed of heavy metals that take several years to decompose.This especially applies to toxic and hazardous waste disposal. Therefore, industries must appropriately dispose of their trash as per the government norms.

Other Steps:

1. Do organic gardening and eat organic food that will be grown without the use of pesticides or insecticide.
2.Do not litter on the ground and do proper disposal of garbage.
3.Buy biodegradable products.
4.For Example: Using cloth bag instead of plastic bag.
5.Try to create awarness in your awarness.

Real Life Innovations

#TEAMTREES

#TEAMTREES is a collaborative fundraiser that raised 20 million U.S. dollars before 2020 to plant 20 million trees. The initiative was started by American YouTubers MrBeast and Mark Rober, and was mostly supported by YouTubers.All donations go to the Arbor Day Foundation, a tree-planting organization that pledges to plant one tree for every U.S. dollar donated.

The Nature Conservancy

The Nature Conservancy is a global environmental nonprofit working to create a world where people and nature can thrive. Founded in the U.S. through grassroots action in 1951, The Nature Conservancy has grown to become one of the most effective and wide-reaching environmental organizations in the world.

Waste Bazaar

Waste Bazaar is a clean-tech providing waste collection and recycling services in Nigeria. They have developed a mobile-phone app that uses geolocation functionalities to connect users to the nearest recycling station, where recyclable waste can be exchanged for “green credits

TONTOTON

TONTOTON is building a system in which the communities play a key role in cleaning their own environment while earning a livelihood through a certified plastic credit system. They work closely and empower local waste pickers to address plastic pollution in Vietnam and Cambodia.

Siklus

Siklus is reinventing the future of retail in Indonesia by delivering refills of everyday needs to people's doors - without plastic waste. They offer an alternative by replacing low value plastic with refill stations, allowing consumers to buy household products in any quantity without plastic packaging.

gCycle

gCycle is tackling sustainability in the nappy industry, which contributes to polluted landfills and waterways. The solution brings the circular economy to nappies and regenerates natural systems. The newest invention is the world’s first patented fully compostable and disposable nappy.
