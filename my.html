WATER

Trees act as natural pollution filters for water .They play a key role in capturing rainwater and reducing the risk of natural disasters like floods and landslides. Trees reduce the amount of storm water runoff, which reduces erosion and pollution in our waterways and may reduce the effects of flooding.According to the UN Food and Agriculture Association, a mature evergreen tree can intercept more than 15,000 litres of water every year.

WILDLIFE

Trees support the lives of many large organisms.Many species of wildlife depend on trees for habitat. Trees provide food, protection, and homes for many birds and mammals.During times of extreme heat or precipitation, animals seek shade and shelter under the trees without being away from their food source; the shade helps them regulate their body temperatures.Without trees, forest creatures would have nowhere to call home.
