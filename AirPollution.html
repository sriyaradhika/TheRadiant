Natural Air

-Protect it to save our future.

The atmosphere of Earth, commonly known as air, is the layer of gases retained by Earth's gravity that surrounds the planet and forms its planetary atmosphere. The atmosphere of Earth protects life on Earth by creating pressure allowing for liquid water to exist on the Earth's surface, absorbing ultraviolet solar radiation, warming the surface through heat retention (greenhouse effect), and reducing temperature extremes between day and night.


Air Pollution

-If we cause it, we will have to pay for it.

Air is composed of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, 0.9% argon.The remaining elements include carbon dioxide, water vapour, hydrogen, and other trace elements. Although gases like carbon dioxide and methane may only exist in small absolute concentrations, their outsized heat-trapping potential as greenhouse gases makes them the major factor in accelerating climate change. Air pollution is caused by a combination of gaseous and particulate pollutants such as carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrogen dioxide emitted from point sources such as factories and motor vehicles that burn fuel. Some gaseous emissions are visible to the eye and sometimes may even diffuse into the atmosphere and become invisible. Particulate pollution, on the other hand, such as soot and black carbon, is always visible.


Air Quality Index (AQI):

-Index for reporting air quality.

  • The higher the AQI value, the greater the level of air pollution and the greater the health concern.


Top 5 Polluted Cities In India(2021):


Faridabad:

AQI : 228 µg/m3

Reason:

Faridabad's source of pollution is its vehicular pollution, construction dust and burning of municipal solid waste in nearby Delhi. Even electroplating units and smoke-emitting industries with the small chimneys lead to pollutants.

Ghaziabad:

AQI : 162 µg/m3

Reason:

Ghaziabad is most polluted because it is located in the Indo-Gangetic plain, and as the winter season approaches, emissions from various activities such as biomass burning etc. get trapped as the inversion layer comes down and the wind becomes very calm.

Gurugram:

AQI : 151 µg/m3

Reason:

Gurugram is home to offices of many big national and international companies due to which there is a high vehicular movement in a small radius of the area resulting in high levels of air pollution. It is also a hub of more than 300 big and small industries.




Noida:

AQI : 147 µg/m3

Reason:

In Noida,the primary causes of air pollution are solid, liquid particles called aerosols & gase from vehicles emissions, construction activities, factories, burning stubble & fossil fuels and wildfire, etc..

Baghpat:

AQI:145 µg/m3

Reason:

In Baghpat,Burning stubble is considered to be an important reason behind pollution of air. It is being told that 29 percent of air pollution is due to which people are facing respiratory problems and irritation in their eyes.


Analysis of Most Polluted Cities in India:

Of the 20 most polluted cities in India, 8 cities are located in Uttar Pradesh, while 5 cities are from Haryana. It is not surprising to see cities from Maharashtra and Bihar in the top 20 rankings, because they are known for their poor air quality. Also, more monitoring devices are required to have adequate air quality data in the particular area, as most cities have limited monitoring stations in India.



All of the ranked cities had average air quality many times above the WHO recommended healthy limit. The worst city on the list, Faridabad, averaged PM2.5 levels over 43-times higher than the WHO recommended limit.Even the best city on the list (ranked 20th), Patna, averaged PM2.5 levels 16-times the WHO recommended limit.
